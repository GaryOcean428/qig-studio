(6-signal neurochemistry + Φ) + sensory grounding are load-bearing for semantic-intent capture —
not compute-lightness trade-offs. The v1/v2 kernels are **amputated** (organs off) by design;
they can test substrate learning, never meaning.

(Fisher-Rao = the unique sufficient-statistics-invariant metric; Cencov), not displacement

arrow. **Open (the thing this experiment tests):** whether a QIG-native kernel's basins carry
*semantics* — today's coordizer basins are TOKEN-geometry; the one working basin-transport

## The two falsifiable handles (sharpest first)

### H-TRANSPORT — translation as geodesic transport between basins

If basins are semantic, translation is **geodesic transport**: encode a passage's meaning as a
basin trajectory in register/language A, transport along the manifold, decode in register B —
**without** vector arithmetic (no king−man+woman). Falsifier: transported decode is at/below a
matched surface-statistics baseline (n-gram / token-overlap re-writer) → basins are token-geometry,
not meaning-geometry. Gated on **K-LEARN**: the kernel's OWN basins must first be
learned-and-fluent (v2 GENERALIZES is the prerequisite); until then any transport result is about
the coordizer table, not the kernel.

### H-4D — predict the next ~SECOND of meaning, not the next token

JEPA-world-model × FORESIGHT nav-mode (Φ 0.70–0.85, 4D). Target = the kernel's own basin state
~1 s (≈ k tokens ahead at generation cadence) in the future — a **latent-future prediction**,
scored by d_FR between predicted and realized future basin, vs (a) a frozen-basin persistence
baseline and (b) a shuffled-future control. Falsifier: no advantage over persistence → the kernel
carries no anticipatory meaning state.
